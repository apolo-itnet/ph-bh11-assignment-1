Pillars of "Bangladesh 2.0"

Our vision is to build a new Bangladesh with the following pillars:

Innovation

Bangladesh is advancing innovation through a growing tech ecosystem and digital transformation initiatives.

Sustainability

Bangladesh is promoting sustainability through green initiatives, renewable energy, and climate-resilient infrastructure.

Infrastructure

Bangladesh's infrastructure is rapidly improving with major investments in transportation, energy, and urban development.

Empowerment

Bangladesh's empowerment initiatives focus on improving gender equality, education, and economic opportunities for all citizens.

The Vision of Future

Bangladesh 2.0 is a vision for a brighter future, where Bangladesh becomes a high-income country by 2041.

Digital Transformation

Creating a nationwide digital ecosystem to enhance connectivity and access to services for all citizens.

Green Energy

Green energy utilizes renewable resources like solar, wind, and hydro to produce clean, sustainable electricity.

Modern Infrastructure

Building state-of-the-art infrastructure to support economic growth and global competitiveness.

Bangladesh Launches New Satellite


Date: 11/01/2025 Time: 10:00 AM

Bangladesh has successfully launched its new satellite, Bangabandhu-1. The satellite, launched aboard a SpaceX Falcon 9 rocket, aims to enhance telecommunications and broadcast services1. It will cover regions such as Bangladesh, its territorial waters, and neighboring countries. This major milestone marks Bangladesh's entry into the space era. The satellite is expected to be operational for 15 years2.

Bangladesh's Padma Bridge


Date: 11/01/2025 Time: 11:00 AM

Bangladesh's Padma Bridge, a marvel of modern engineering, is dramatically boosting the nation's connectivity and economy. Since its opening in June 2022, it has collected over Tk 2,061.97 crore in tolls from nearly 1.6 crore vehicles2. Built entirely with the country's resources, the 6.15-km bridge links Dhaka with 21 southwestern districts, benefiting millions. The project, costing Tk 30,193 crore, symbolizes national pride and resilience2. It is set to impact trade, regional development, and socio-economic growth for years to come.

Bangladesh Metro Rail Project (DMTCL)


Date: 11/01/2025 Time: 11:30 AM

Bangladesh Dhaka Metro Rail Project total cost of Tk 22,000 crore. Officially known as the Mass Rapid Transit (MRT) Line-6, metro rail service is expected to open half of its 20.10 kilometers by the end of 2019. Once completed, the service would carry 60,000 passengers/hour. 20km Metro Rail route will be constructed from Uttara to Bangladesh Bank having 16 stops. The project will save of Tk 200 billion/ year, equivalent to 1.5% gross domestic production (GDP) and 17% of the total tax revenue.
